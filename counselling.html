Counselling

What do we mean by "Counselling"?

The Counselling setting allows for issues to be explored in a safe, non-judgmental place. It encourages the client to look at situations that are of concern to them and view them from many perspectives before deciding on a specific course of action.

In the Counselling relationship the emphasis is on listening to and acknowledging the difficulties that the client is experiencing at this time. The type of counselling used is non-directive and client led. Counselling is not something we 'do' to another person.

It is an ongoing process that goes beyond the intervention of an hour a week between counsellor and client. While not with the counsellor the client is learning to adapt, cope and work on implementing changes, great and small.

We are looking to empower our clients with skills to take forward in to the future rather than provide a permanent crutch.

Counselling in Context

Our aim is that the client should feel heard and understood. Our counsellors are trained to the highest standards; are very experienced and are in constant Clinical Supervision to ensure that the client gets only the very best.

Areas covered include trauma, bereavement, relationships, sexual abuse, confidence building, weight management, and habit control.
