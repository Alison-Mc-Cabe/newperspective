Clinical Hypnotherapy

What is Clinical Hypnotherapy?

Clinical Hypnotherapy is a gentle relaxing way to access the underlying processes that motivate and generate our thoughts and behaviours. With 'negative' thoughts or behaviours it allows us to re-align ourselves to think and behave in a more positive way.

What is Hypnotheraphy

Hypnotherapy is a gentle, safe, relaxing, powerful way to access the unconscious mind to allow sustained change to take place easily and effectively.

What can Hypnotherapy achieve?

  • Clinical Hypnotherapy assists you to make the changes you really want, yet still find difficult.
  • We enable you to make changes in behaviours, habits, and family patterns through relaxation, thereby reducing resistance.
  • It helps you to move forward, in positive, creative, manageable and sustainable ways.

What is hypnotherapy?

Hypnotherapy uses trance state explicitly. Hypnosis is a natural state of mind to everyone. Hypnosis is simply a way of relaxing and setting aside the conscious mind. It is a pleasant, gentle, often liberating experience. Afterward the person is refreshed and immediately ready to get on with their day. People may worry about being out of control while in trance or of being 'brainwashed'.
The client's moral and ethical code will always protect them from suggestions and influences that violate their beliefs and values.

We often seek professional help because we have run out of conscious resources. When we are stuck, we don't know what we need or where to find it. Trance offers an opportunity to resolve the problem because it bypasses the conscious mind and makes unconscious resources available. It is our unconscious or sub conscious mind that runs our patterns and habits so that's where we need to install changes and refinements for sustained change.

Hypnotheraphy in Context

Everyone has a rich personal history, filled with experience and resources that can be drawn on. It contains all the material needed to make changes, if only you can get at it. Trance is the ideal state of mind to explore and recover our unique internal resources. The ultimate goal of any therapy is for the client to become resourceful again in his or her own right.
