Neuro Linguistic Programming

What is Neuro Linguistic Programming (NLP)

NLP is short for Neuro Linguistic Programming. It studies how the mind works, with verifiable and sometimes astonishing results. It is a powerful yet gentle approach to overcoming life's difficulties. There are many accounts of people whose lives have been changed and whose dreams became realities by tapping into their own inner power to change with NLP.

Neuro . Linguistic . Programming .

  • Neuro: Nervous system through which experience is received then processed through the five senses.
  • Linguistic: Language and non-verbal communication systems through which neural representations are coded, ordered and given meaning.
  • Programming: the ability to organise our communication and neurological systems to achieve specific, desired goals and results.

When is it used?

The field of NLP is based on the realization that we create much of our experience by the specific ways we see, hear and feel things in our mind/body; what is usually lumped together and called 'thinking'. So something that excites and energises one person may terrorize another depending on past experiences.

NLP has grown out of the study of the mental processes of those who do something exceptionally well, or who have completely recovered from some difficulty. Most of us were taught to think of exceptional skills as the result of inborn abilities, traits and talents. NLP demonstrates that by thinking about our abilities as learned skills, we open the door of understanding our selves and our skills more easily and our ability of teaching them to and learning them from others.

NLP offers effective techniques for a wide range of problems including: unwanted habits, guilt, grief, weight management, abuse (both emotional and sexual), criticism, shame, stage-fright and phobias. It also offers ways to enhance self-esteem and improve relationships, enabling people to become more independent. It creates positive motivation, eliminates allergic responses, and promotes self healing.

NLP in the therapeutic sense looks at our internal maps and triggers. It could be said to be 'behavioural change therapy'. The practitioner, along with the client looks at the patterns of the behaviour. Together they create strategies to alter the limiting behaviour so that a more positive response comes about.

NLP in Context

  • NLP pre-supposes that the resources and individual needs to effect change are already within them.
  • NLP pre-supposes that the positive worth of the individual is held constant, while the value and appropriateness of internal and/or external behaviour is questioned.
  • NLP pre-supposes that there is a positive intention motivating all behaviour and a context in which all behaviour has value.

NLP offers a systematic set of concepts and methods for understanding and changing human experience.

Using NLP we know how to explore a person's thinking in a precise way that actually allows us to take on that person's skills and abilities if we so choose.
